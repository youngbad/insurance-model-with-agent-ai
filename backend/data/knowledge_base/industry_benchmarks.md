# Industry Benchmarks and Reference Data

## Industry Classification and Risk Profiles

### Technology Sector
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $250,000 - $500,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 35-55%
- **Claim Frequency**: 0.8-2.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Cyber liability, E&O, property damage, employment practices
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.3-0.8

### Financial Services
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $400,000 - $1,200,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 40-60%
- **Claim Frequency**: 0.5-1.5 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Professional liability, cyber, crime/fidelity
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 10-50 (due to managed assets)

### Healthcare
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $100,000 - $200,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 60-75%
- **Claim Frequency**: 2.0-5.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Medical malpractice, general liability, workers comp
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.5-1.5

### Manufacturing - Light
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $150,000 - $350,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 55-70%
- **Claim Frequency**: 3.0-6.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Workers comp, products liability, property
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.8-2.0

### Manufacturing - Heavy / Industrial
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $200,000 - $500,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 65-80%
- **Claim Frequency**: 5.0-10.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Workers comp, property, pollution liability
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 1.5-4.0

### Construction
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $200,000 - $400,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 65-85%
- **Claim Frequency**: 5.0-12.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Workers comp, general liability, completed operations
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.3-0.8

### Retail
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $120,000 - $250,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 55-70%
- **Claim Frequency**: 2.0-4.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Slip & fall, products liability, property, crime
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.4-1.2

### Transportation / Logistics
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $150,000 - $300,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 70-85%
- **Claim Frequency**: 4.0-8.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: Commercial auto, cargo, workers comp
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 0.5-2.0

### Hospitality
- **Average Revenue per Employee**: $50,000 - $120,000
- **Typical Loss Ratio**: 60-75%
- **Claim Frequency**: 3.0-6.0 per 100 employees
- **Primary Risks**: General liability, property, liquor liability, workers comp
- **Typical Assets/Revenue Ratio**: 1.0-4.0

## Country Risk Factors

### Tier 1 - Low Additional Risk (Factor: 1.0)
United States, Canada, Germany, Switzerland, Australia, Japan, UK, Netherlands, Scandinavia

### Tier 2 - Moderate Additional Risk (Factor: 1.1-1.3)
France, Italy, Spain, Poland, Czech Republic, South Korea, Singapore, Israel

### Tier 3 - Higher Additional Risk (Factor: 1.3-1.6)
Brazil, Mexico, China, India, South Africa, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, Argentina

### Tier 4 - High Additional Risk (Factor: 1.6-2.0)
Russia, Venezuela, Nigeria, Pakistan, Indonesia (certain regions)

### Natural Catastrophe Zones
- **High Hurricane Risk**: Florida, Gulf Coast, Caribbean
- **High Earthquake Risk**: California, Pacific Northwest, Japan, Turkey
- **High Flood Risk**: Bangladesh, parts of Southeast Asia, low-lying coastal areas
- **High Wildfire Risk**: California, Australia, Mediterranean Europe

## Regulatory Environment by Industry

### Highly Regulated Industries (Higher Compliance Costs)
- Financial Services: SEC, FINRA, state insurance regulators
- Healthcare: HIPAA, CMS, state medical boards
- Pharmaceuticals: FDA, DEA
- Aviation: FAA, EASA
- Nuclear: NRC
- Food Processing: USDA, FDA

### Impact on Insurance
- Highly regulated industries face higher D&O and regulatory defense costs
- Non-compliance can trigger coverage disputes
- Regulatory fines typically not covered under standard policies
